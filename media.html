Law of Demand

Definition:

A microeconomic law that states, all other factors being equal, as the price of a good
or service increases, consumer demand for the good or service will decrease, and vice
versa. The law of demand says that the higher the price, the lower the quantity
demanded, because consumers' opportunity cost to acquire that good or service
increases, and they must make more tradeoffs to acquire the more expensive product.
